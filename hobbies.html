Piano/Instruments

For my current skill level, I feel like I've been playing piano for too long, so I won't tell you when I started. I think I'm so bad because there was a time where I practiced at the most maybe 5 minutes a week, and still continued to progress through levels. But, after I started doing another activity which I will not disclose, I started appreciating piano lessons and playing piano itself a lot more, so now it's just something that I like doing. One week when I was trying to speed-learn a piece, I think I played too much and too often that I messed up my wrists a bit, so now I can't play too long without my wrists aching. It's still fun though, and I like playing songs in my own time. I wish I played more during quarantine and the summer where I really did have a lot of free time, but at the same time it's not like I'll be stopped from playing piano later on. I want to be able to get to a point where I can improv short tunes or play songs by ear, but I've got no chance as of now since I have virtually no knowledge on music theory and I really suck at ear training. Other than piano, I play 3 other instruments (not including recorder): flute, trumpet, and ukulele. I started playing flute in grade 6 when we were given a choice in music, and I chose it because I thought it'd be quiet enough to be hidden by all the other instruments; that way no one would notice if I made any mistakes. Not only that though, I think the flute is a very pretty instrument and it was fun playing it. I played it through grade 6 and 7, and played it in grade 8 for band as well. In grade 8 for music classes, I switched to trumpet. While trumpet is fun to play, I'm not great at it because I can't push enough air to go up higher. I basically switched because I wanted to push the buttons, but I stuck with it through the year and now I miss playing both flute and trumpet. During quarantine though, my brother bought himself a ukulele but said it was too small. I'd been wanting a ukulele so I got to take it, and I started to learn on my own. As of August I knew three songs, and still know a grand total of three songs. My fingers hurt for a while when I first started to play but like every other instrument I mentioned, it's fun to play. Due to my current piano level I'm expected to do music theory so I do online classes, and I don't know if it's just me but I actually kind of like learning music theory. I'm hoping that I can get it ingrained in my head so I can recognize different things about what I play. While I'd like to get better at the instruments I already play, there's also a bunch more that I'd still like to play, so I'm hoping that I'll have the opportunity to later on. Back to top

Art

So something I like to do in my free time is draw, and even though I'm laughably slow at it, it's still something that I enjoy doing. To be honest, I'd call myself mediocre or decent at best, but one of the best things about art is that it's not hard to improve through practice and repeitition. Everyone starts somewhere, so if you feel like you aren't that good you shouldn't worry about it too much; just draw whatever you like to draw and you'll improve naturally! In fact, just being able to draw whatever I want and not taking it seriously is probably what makes art enjoyable for me, and why I wouldn't consider
it for a career. I already take breaks that last multiple weeks, so if I had to draw often knowing that the final product actually mattered, I would probably stop enjoying art as much as I do.
To the left is just a work in progress drawing that I don't really plan on finishing, and to the right is a collaboration drawing between a couple of friends and I during french class. I drew the background for this website too, don't look to closely. See, people tend to say they dislike art because they're bad at it, but since art is so subjective I feel like your skill level isn't extremely important and you can just have fun messing around and drawing something dumb.
I usually just do sketches because it's most convienient, but every so often I'll do watercolour paintings too. Thing is, I can't paint without reference because I'm not great at colouring. It'll turn out alright if I do have a reference and spend enough time on it though. The picture with the paintings saying
“ call me a baker 'cause I be getting this bread ” were made in art club for no reason at all, other than the fact that we thought it'd be fun, and the "call me a baker" half was done by a very very talented friend of mine. It's always been fun to draw with other people because usually no one has any expectations. Drawing doesn't have to be taken seriously, and that's what I think makes art enjoyable, regardless of whether or not you think you're good at it. If you want a fun drawing game where you don't have to be good, you should try skribbl.io if you haven't already B-).

Shows

Over the years my tv watch-time has gradually decreased, so I guess it was a bit of a lie to put "Shows" as the heading; just consider it to be about any form of content you would watch. For a while now I've been spending more hours infront of a screen than I'd be proud to admit, and a lot of it came from watching Youtube and recently, Twitch. I won't mention any of the creators I watch for no particular reason, but I can mention some shows I've watched instead. Compared to other people I don't think I watch that much anime, and it usually takes a lot for me to start a series. I'm perhaps a bit basic when it comes to what I watch too, since I usually just watch whatever's popular or was recommended to me. I've watched animes of different genres, but I usually just like light-hearted stories with fun characters who have funny interactions. To the right, you'll find screenshots of my favourite moment in “My Hero Academia”. Other than anime, I've tried some dramas like “Boys Over Flowers” and “The Prince Of Tennis”, but ultimately was unable to take them seriously. No offence to whoever worked hard on the shows. I might still watch them though, because when I watch things I just like to turn off my brain for a while and enjoy something mindlessly. Back to top

Reading

Just like my tv watch-time, my time spent reading has decreased too, just not gradually at all. I do still enjoy it though, and will probably stay up reading if I find a good book. Usually every summer I re-read the entire Harry Potter series to try to reassure myself that I didn't waste money buying the set, but I got so lazy I didn't do it this year. Some cool people got me the Hunger Games prequel though, so I spent a couple days on that, and I'm currently on chapter 6 of “Sapiens A Brief History of Humankind”. My lack of reading is only if you count traditional reading though, because during quarantine I made a habit of spending an unfortunate amount of time reading Webtoons and manhwa. I'm not too picky about books, and I remember thinking that there's never been a book that I've read and didn't like, but as of today I can tell you that two books you should avoid reading are “The Selection” and “The School of Good and Evil”, though you could always read them if you wanted a laugh.
